# Material 3 Expressive Co-design

Co-design supports innovation by involving the people who use a product or service in the creation process. It goes beyond traditional user research by treating community members as active partners in design decisions.

## 1. Principles of Co-design

- **Build With, Not For**: Prioritize engaging with people of varied backgrounds and experiences.
- **Ongoing Process**: Building with everyone is a continuous commitment, not a one-time event.
- **Prioritize Overlooked Communities**: Actively engage with communities that are often overlooked to build products that benefit everyone.
- **Validate & Discover**: Validate assumptions and discover new opportunities by conducting market research with a broad range of people.

## 2. Questions to Consider

Use these questions throughout the development process to ensure meaningful engagement and inclusivity.

### Addressing User Needs & Contexts
- What are some opportunities to address user needs keeping in mind attributes of people and their contexts?
- Who else can benefit from the product?
- What are their needs?
- How are you making sure overlooked communities are included in ongoing testing?

### Engaging Communities Early & Often
- How are you engaging community members and experts early and often throughout the development process?
- How will their feedback shape key decisions about feature priorities, functionality, and messaging?
- How can you continue to apply these insights as you move from the ideation phase to other stages of product development?
- What are some opportunities to gain even more insights?

### Addressing Risks & Harms
- What are the risks and potential harms if you exclude people from overlooked communities?
- How can you address them?

## 3. Co-creation Strategies

- **Flatten Hierarchies**: Ensure all participants feel their input is valued equally.
- **Mutual Benefit**: Ensure the engagement provides value back to the community.
- **Diverse Prototyping**: Use varied methods (sketching, role-playing, physical mockups) to explore solutions beyond digital screens.
- **Iterative Refinement**: Cycle back to the community frequently to verify that refinements still meet their core needs.
